Navigating the Frontier Model Arms Race

As AI models reach unprecedented capabilities, geopolitics mirrors Cold War nuclear dynamics. Conflict is avoided when the Information Entropy of the system is low and Coupling is high. If the Perceived Payoff of the status quo remains higher than the Maximum Potential Payoff of an escalation, the system remains in a metastable peace.

02 Kahn's Escalation Ladder

If the Metastable Peace framework fails, the system enters the state-transition model of Herman Kahn's 44-rung ladder. Each threshold represents a discrete state with increasing negative payoffs and decreasing reversibility.

The conflict begins with official complaints and low-level harassment. In game-theoretic terms for Frontier Models, these are signals intended to communicate resolve without committing significant compute resources. Examples include trade restrictions on niche hardware or diplomatic censures regarding data scraping.
Actors begin explicitly demonstrating capabilities to enforce deterrence. This involves significant financial and compute allocation. For AI models, this resembles cyber probes, intentional manipulation of open-source datasets (poisoning), and public demonstrations of dual-use models capable of bypassing standard safeguards.
The "Burn Bridges" threshold is crossed. Mutual coupling drops near zero. In the AI context, this represents physical sabotage of hyperscale data centers, deployment of unaligned autonomous agents specifically designed to attack rival infrastructure, and the complete severing of global technological supply chains.
Spasm warfare. At this stage, actors attempt to achieve Escalation Dominance through overwhelming force, ignoring collateral damage. This involves the mass deployment of self-improving, unconstrained algorithmic attacks resulting in catastrophic infrastructural, economic, and societal damage globally. Mutually Assured Destruction is realized.
